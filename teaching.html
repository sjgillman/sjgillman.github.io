Teaching & Outreach


I believe part of being an effective scientist is sharing your knowledge with
others through outreach, mentorship, and an openness with analyzes and data whenever possible.
As a first-generation student in college, there were mistakes, bumps, and detours along my path
into the world of ecology. I credit the many people who took the time to mentor me along the way
and of course the many people who make understanding analyze, through online tutorials, accessible
to new students. That is why I prioritize being available to students that I teach by pointing them
to different sources to find jobs, reading cover letters and CVs, and workshops ranging from DNA
extractions for microbiome research, to training them in R and other statistical software. Additionally,
because I see and value the potential advancement open-source data can bring to ecology, I ensure all
data and scripts involved in published manuscripts and tutorials I make for students are available on
platforms such as GitHubZenodo.


Principles of Ecology Lab: BI 210L

Teaching Assistant, Biology Department, Northern Michigan University 2018-2019

As a teaching assistant for the Principles of Ecology lab, I was also involved in course development for each of the labs. I had the opportunity to introduce undergraduate students to a variety of concepts including ecological succession, conducting laboratory and field research, scientific reading & writing, as well as teaching students to identify the local flora and fauna.

General biology for Non-majors: BSC 1005

Teaching Assistant, Biology Department, Florida State University 2012-2013

During my junior and senior years of undergradate school, I was one of five taching assistants for a class of 400. My role as a teaching assistant was to answer any questions during the lectures, hold office hours to tutor students on topic ranging from human reproduction to archaeology, grade, and hold presentations and reviews for exams.

Workshops: DNA Extraction and Statistical Analysis for Microbiome Research

Undergraduate and graduate workshops, Northern Michigan University, 2019 & 2020

As part of my masters, I trained undergraduates and new graduates in the proper protocols required for microbial DNA extractions using DNeasy PowerSoil kits. This allowed undergraduates to undertake supervised research and ensured new graduate students were prepared for their research projects.

In addition to training students in labratory protocols, I provided tutorials on the bioinformatic and analytical pipeline for wildlife microbial research in QIIME2, GALAXY, and R.
